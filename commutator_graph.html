Commutator graph¶

This tutorial will illustrate how to use paulie to analyse short term dynamics of quantum systems. The Hamiltonian of the system is described by a linear combination of Pauli strings \(H = \sum_{P \in \mathcal{G}} c_P P\) where \(\mathcal{G}\) is the generator set. The commutator graph of an \(n\)-qubit system has the \(4^n\) Pauli strings as vertices and there exists and edge \((P,Q)\) if there exists a generator \(G \in \mathcal{G}\) s.t. \([P,G] \propto Q\). While the anticommutation graph is isomorphic for generator sets with isomorphic DLA’s and only capture the long term dynamics, the commutator graph captures short term dynamics. We can plot the commutator graph

from paulie.common.pauli_string_factory import get_pauli_string as p
generators = p(["XI", "ZI", "IX", "IZ", "ZZ"], n=2)
vertices, edges = generators.get_commutator_graph()
plot_graph(vertices, edges)
