Commutator graph¶

This tutorial will illustrate how to use paulie to analyse short term dynamics of quantum systems. The Hamiltonian of the system is described by a linear combination of Pauli strings \(H = \sum_{P \in \mathcal{G}} c_P P\) where \(\mathcal{G}\) is the generator set. The commutator graph of an \(n\)-qubit system has the \(4^n\) Pauli strings as vertices and there exists and edge \((P,Q)\) if there exists a generator \(G \in \mathcal{G}\) s.t. \([P,G] \prop Q\). While the anticommutation graph is isomorphic for generator sets with isomorphic DLA’s and only capture the long term dynamics, the commutator graph captures short term dynamics.
